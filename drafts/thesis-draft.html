One of my first PC memories is sitting in front of the family PC running Windows 98 doodling in Paint. I don‘t have any recollection of surfing the web at this age. This changed with me getting my very first laptop in 2002 – I was 10 or 11 years old at that point. I‘ve just talked to my dad about it – unlike me, he has a good memory. He bought the laptop on sale at a Real near Berlin in 2002. With this I also got my own connection to the internet at some point – probably by still plugging in a cable from the router into the LAN port of my Laptop. With a personal device and a connection to the internet I was well on my way in becoming a very online teen. With the spread of social media and microblogging in the mid 2000s I had a cringy MySpace page and was later very active on tumblr. Both sites had the possibility to customize your page or blog. 1Here is a really nice segment about the shift from personalized social media profiles like MySpace to a unified visual apperance. EP 23 Neverpost – Looks Great: The End of Personalization I really enjoyed all creativity people had with styling and personalizing their little web spaces, yet I never did that for myself, because I was very afraid of coding. With MySpace I‘m not so sure anymore how I customized it – but like tumblr it also had a code editor. With tumblr however I vividly remember this moment of me opening the custom css panel many times and closing it again and again in fear. In the end I always used themes – not that this is bad or worse in any way from doing it yourself. However for me personally it had somewhat of an sour aftertaste, because I always wanted to make my own stuff but was not able to out of fear and feelings of inadequacy. While researching this thesis and thinking about my relation to code a lot of memories from my childhood and early teens came back to me. I remembered why I originally used to be so afraid of coding – it was the Informationstechnischer Grundkurs I had in 2005, which was seventh grade for me. 1
